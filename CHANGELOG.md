# Viral10K Changelog

## 2026-03-20 — 3D Showcase Enhancements

### Summary
Session focused on iterating the 3D sphere showcase generated by `generate_3d_showcase()`. Started with visual polish, explored several layout concepts, and landed on a cleaner UX approach: filter dropdowns instead of spatial grouping.

---

### Changes to `viral10k.py`

#### Fox orbit tightened
The orbiting fox icon now hugs the sphere surface with no visible gap.
- Orbit radius multiplier: `1.65x` → `1.08x`
- Vertical bob amplitude: `35%` → `12%` of orbit radius

#### Industry color-coding on spheres
Added `CLIENT_INDUSTRIES` dict at module level mapping ~30 clients to one of five industries:

| Industry | Color |
|---|---|
| Legal | #f5c842 (gold) |
| Mortgage & Finance | #00c896 (teal) |
| Real Estate | #b06cff (purple) |
| Food & Hospitality | #ff6b35 (orange) |
| Creators & Media | #00f2ff (cyan) |
| Other | #888888 (grey) |

Spheres are now colored by industry instead of platform.

#### Filter dropdowns
Two dropdowns added top-right of the showcase:
- **FMO filter**: All Posts / FMO Produced / Client Posts
- **Industry filter**: All Industries + dynamically populated from scan data

Filtering sets `mesh.visible` on spheres and syncs fox sprite visibility. Raycaster tooltip hover only tests visible spheres. A live count ("N posts shown") appears below the dropdowns.

---

### Approaches explored and abandoned

1. **Galaxy layout** — group same-client hits into cell clusters, swirl clusters in industry galaxies. Too complex to view; clusters overlapped at any reasonable zoom level.
2. **Two-hemisphere layout** — FMO vs Client Posts as two halves of the scene, industry sub-clusters within each. Cluster spacing math never resolved cleanly; abandoned.

Final decision: revert to original random cloud placement, use UI controls (dropdowns) for grouping/filtering instead of spatial layout.
